## What IQ Actually Measures

IQ tests measure a specific bundle of cognitive abilities:

- **Pattern recognition** — seeing rules in sequences of shapes, numbers, or symbols
- **Logical reasoning** — deducing relationships from limited information
- **Spatial reasoning** — mentally rotating objects, understanding 2D/3D relationships
- **Working memory** — how much information you can juggle in your head at once
- **Processing speed** — how fast you can handle simple tasks accurately

That's it. IQ does *not* measure creativity, emotional intelligence, practical wisdom, musical ability, or whether you're good at talking to people at parties.

A genius painter with terrible pattern-recognition skills might score average on IQ. A software engineer who can blast through matrix reasoning might score highly but be terrible at reading a room. The test has a scope. It measures what it measures.

## The IQ Scale (And Where You Actually Rank)

| IQ Range | Percentile | Roughly 1 in... | What It Means |
|---|---|---|---|
| **130+** | Top 2% | 50 people | Mensa territory. You process complex patterns faster than most. |
| **120–129** | Top 9% | 10 people | Strong analytical thinker. STEM fields feel natural. |
| **110–119** | Top 25% | 4 people | Above average. Good problem-solver, quick learner. |
| **90–109** | 25th–75th | 2 people | Right in the middle. Half the population sits here. |
| **80–89** | Bottom 25% | 4 people | Below average. May struggle with abstract reasoning, but perfectly functional in most life tasks. |
| **Below 80** | Bottom 10% | 10+ people | May need accommodations for complex academic or analytical tasks. |

Here's the thing nobody tells you: **the difference between 115 and 125 is significant.** It's not "basically the same." A 115 is "smart, good with logic." A 125 is "this person can learn abstract math without wanting to cry."

The difference between 100 and 110? Less dramatic. Both are solid. Both can do most jobs. IQ matters most at the extremes.

## "Is My IQ Good?" — The Real Answer

If you scored **100**: You're average. That's not an insult — half the world is average. You can do most jobs, learn most skills, and live a completely normal life. The world runs on people with 100 IQ.

If you scored **115**: You're bright. You probably picked up math faster than classmates. You spot patterns others miss. Most professional jobs fit you comfortably.

If you scored **130+**: You're in the top 2%. This doesn't make you a genius automatically, but it means your brain handles complexity with less friction. You probably found school boring because it moved too slow.

If you scored **below 90**: You're below average. This is also not a disaster. Plenty of successful people score here. IQ measures specific skills — not your worth, work ethic, or ability to build a good life.

## Percentiles: The Number That Actually Matters More Than IQ

Your percentile tells you more than your raw score.

- **IQ 100 = 50th percentile** → You're smarter than 50% of people tested.
- **IQ 115 = 84th percentile** → Smarter than 84%.
- **IQ 130 = 98th percentile** → Smarter than 98%.
- **IQ 145 = 99.9th percentile** → Smarter than 999 out of 1,000.

Percentiles are easier to grasp than IQ points. "I'm in the 94th percentile" lands harder than "I scored 124."

## Can IQ Change?

Short answer: **slightly, not dramatically.**

Your IQ is about 50–80% genetic. The rest comes from education, nutrition, sleep, and whether you grew up in a stimulating environment.

You won't go from 95 to 130 by doing Sudoku. But you *can* optimize what you have:

- **Sleep 8 hours** — sleep deprivation drops IQ-equivalent performance by 10–15 points
- **Exercise** — cardio increases blood flow to the brain, measurably improving processing speed
- **Learn deeply** — learning complex skills (math, languages, music) builds the neural networks IQ tests measure
- **Stop multitasking** — context-switching destroys the working memory IQ tests reward

Think of it like height: you're not getting taller at 25. But you can learn to stand straighter.
